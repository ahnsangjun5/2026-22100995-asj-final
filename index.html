It refers to the illegal act of hijacking a moving vehicle, train, aircraft, or other means of transportation. There is a theory that the word "hijack" originated during the U.S.Prohibition era from the greeting "Hi, Jack," which was used when gangs robbed trucks of illegally manufactured liquor or hijacked smuggling vessels at sea. However, as aircraft hijacking incidents occurred overwhelmingly more frequently than other forms of hijacking, the term began to be used specifically to refer to aircraft hijacking.

運行中の車両、電車、航空機、その他の輸送手段に対する違法 な拉致行為をいう。本来、ハイジャックは米国の今週法時代、主に貨物車に載せられた違法製造酒類を強奪するギャングの路上商道行為や海上で酒類の密輸船を拉致する際に渡される挨拶「Hi,Jack」から派生した単語という説がある。使い始めた。
